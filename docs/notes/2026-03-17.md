## Key Insights

- Calibration quality depends more on **data quality** than quantity
- More images ≠ better calibration
- Robust filtering is essential
- Better camera calibration does not always improve final plane fit
- Many images are rejected due to:
  - checkerboard detection failure
  - poor laser coverage on board

## Important Observations

- Only ~16/40 images were actually usable
- Rejection is not necessarily bad — improves final result
- Laser must clearly intersect checkerboard for usable data
- Reflections and noise affect ridge extraction

## Limitations

- Camera RMS still above target (<0.5 px)
- Checkerboard detection unreliable in some cases
- Laser stripe not perfectly continuous (dot pattern)
